Ernesto Trajano de Lima Neto

Professor da Universidade Federal do Ceará (Instituto Universidade Virtual) desde 2014, possui graduação em Música (Piano -- Bacharelado) pela Universidade Federal da Paraíba (1998), mestrado em Informática pela Universidade Federal da Paraíba - Campus II, atual Universidade Federal de Campina Grande (2001) e doutorado em Ciência da Computação pela Universidade Federal de Pernambuco (2007), com passagem pelo Instituto Austríaco de Pesquisas em Inteligência Artificial (ÖFAI). Possui experiência na área de Ciência da Computação, com ênfase em Computação Musical e Inteligência Artificial Aplicada, atuando principalmente nos seguintes temas: análise musical assistida por computador, análise automática da expressividade (expressive music performance), recuperação da informação musical e aplicações de técnicas de Inteligência Artificial no domínio da Música. Antes de sua entrada na Academia, trabalhou como Analista de Tecnologia da Informação, possuindo experiência na análise e no desenvolvimento de sistemas, em particular no levantamento de requisitos, arquitetura de software, implementação e testes.                                                 5. 2014-2017. KIGB: Knowing and interacting while gaming for the blind
                                                  Descrição: The development and enhancement of cognitive skills in people who are blind has been highly demanded in the literature. Digital technology targeting this need is widely desired and demanded. There is also a need beyond the technology development. Usability and cognitive impact evaluation studies of technology for people with visual disability are claimed and required for better understanding and adequate and pertinent use. This proposal is targeting this need and demand. We focus on Knowing and Interacting while Gaming for the Blind, KIGB. We intend to design, implement and evaluate a multimodal mobile videogame application for enhancing cognition in people with visual disabilities. Thus the focal point is mobile gaming for further developing cognitive processes such as mental models, cognitive spatial structures, and navigation skills in people with visual disabilities. Mobile software evaluation, usability evaluation and impact evaluation will be a key in this project. By implementing KIGB we aim at producing a mobile gaming computer application that is usable, understandable and effective. Finally, the most global purpose of the KIGB project is to contribute from the computer science side to better develop the intellect of people who are blind, to help them to be more autonomous and independent in their life and their relations with people and surroundings, and thus helping them to be more integrated and included in our society and culture..
