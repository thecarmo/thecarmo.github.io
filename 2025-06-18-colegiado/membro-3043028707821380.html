                                                          1. 2010-Atual. Radio Resource Management for Service Quality Provision and Device-to-Device Communication
                                                            Descrição: To attend the growing demand for data services, wireless systems are required to evolve continuously. In its International Mobile Telecommunications-Advanced initiative, the International Telecommunication Union established a set of requirements to be met by future wireless systems, such as 3rd Generation Partnership Project?s Long Term Evolution-Advanced. In order to fulfill the established requirements, Multiple-Input-Multiple-Output (MIMO) techniques are a key technology. Indeed, MIMO techniques can boost system capacity, but they put new challenges to Radio Resource Management (RRM) due to the use of the spatial dimension. Thus, RRM techniques need to be further developed, especially for Quality-of-Service (QoS) provision where important questions on how to best share resources among spatially multiplexed users still arise. Besides that, standardization bodies, industry, and academia are continuously researching new wireless technologies, among which Device-to-Device (D2D) communication within cellular systems appears as a relatively new area that might offer potentially high benefits for future wireless networks in terms of performance and market opportunities. In this project we aim at investigating RRM techniques for service QoS provision in multi-antenna systems and for D2D communication.. Situação: Em andamento; Natureza: Pesquisa. Integrantes: Ricardo Brauner dos santos - Coordenador. Financiador(es): Telefonaktiebolaget LM Ericsson - Remuneração.
                                                            Membro: Ricardo Brauner dos santos.
                                                          2. 2008-2010. Advanced Radio Resource Management for Multiservice OFDMA Systems
                                                            Descrição: Traditional RRM functionalities like subcarrier and power allocations work well in OFDMA system up to the network load limits. However, these functionalities are not enough effective in congestion situations, such as when overload and/or outage occur. Furthermore, the particular scenario of co-existence between best effort and strict delay sensitive services, such as Web Browsing and Voice over IP (VoIP), is of paramount interest in upcoming OFDMA-based wireless systems and a main challenge is the support of a high capacity VoIP service, regarding its tight delay requirements and intermittently generated small packets. Managing multiple services for high spectral efficiency and quality of service is the key driver for this project. A second subject covered in the project is Distributed Antenna Systems (DAS). In DAS, the radio frequency and control units are split and multiple remote antennas are distributed over a large area and connected to their controller by a fast link. DAS have been proposed mainly as a means of coverage extension in 2G and 3G systems. In this project we aim at investigating interplays and potential gains of RRM for OFDMA in the DAS context.. Situação: Concluído; Natureza: Pesquisa. Integrantes: Ricardo Brauner dos santos - Integrante / Francisco Rodrigo Porto Cavalcanti - Integrante / Francisco Rafael Marques Lima - Integrante / Walter Cruz Freitas - Coordenador / Elvis M. G. Stancanelli - Integrante. Financiador(es): Ericsson Telecomunicações - Matriz - Auxílio financeiro.
                                                            Membro: Ricardo Brauner dos santos.
                                                          3. 2006-2008. Radio Resource Allocation for OFDMA-based Multiuser Multicell Wireless Systems
                                                            Descrição: Future broadband nomadic/mobile wireless systems based on OFDM technology have been receiving an intense effort from the scientific community to develop concepts and to assure the feasibility of such systems. In fact, the development of high performance OFDM-based wireless radio access technologies to achieve high speed and high quality transmission in a reliable and spectrally efficient manner constitutes a key challenging undertaking. Efficient radio resource allocation is essential to provide Quality-of-Service (QoS) for wireless networks. Rather, the problem of allocating available resources in a multi-cell OFDMA scenario is much more challenging, since time diversity, frequency diversity as well as multi-user diversity should be efficiently exploited in the time, frequency and multi-user domain, respectively. It is straightforward to see that the RRM problematic assumes a multi-dimensional characteristic in wireless systems based on OFDM radio access technology. Therefore, there is plenty of room for performance evaluation of OFDMA-based networks in a multi-user multi-cell approach. There exist a number of open issues to be assessed in this proposal that can be coherently divided into three main objects of study: (i) Multi-user Multi-cell Wireless System-level aspects: deployment, signaling, measurements and feedback architectures; (ii) RRM algorithms conception and development; and (iii) Link-level issues: relationship of RRM techniques to physical layer and link-to-system interface designs. The research strategy will be conducted in two main fronts: theoretical analysis and systemic simulations. Situação: Concluído; Natureza: Pesquisa. G. Stancanelli - Integrante. Financiador(es): Ericsson Telecomunicações - Matriz - Auxílio financeiro.
